Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar

Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar (1891-1956) was a social reformer, jurist, and the chief architect of the Indian Constitution. He dedicated his life to fighting against social discrimination, inequality, and the caste system. Dr. Ambedkar was born into a lower-caste family and faced discrimination throughout his life. He played a significant role in uplifting marginalized communities and worked towards their social and political empowerment.

As the chairman of the drafting committee, Dr. Ambedkar was instrumental in shaping the Constitution of India. He advocated for equality, fundamental rights, and social justice. His efforts led to the inclusion of various provisions to safeguard the rights of marginalized communities, abolish untouchability, and promote social equality.
